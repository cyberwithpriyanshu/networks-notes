# Networks

## What are Networks?

A **network** is a collection of interconnected devices. These devices are connected to each other to share (send or receive) some kind of data. Moreover, they can also be connected to share resources.

### Components of a Network

1. **Node** — Each connected device in the network is known as a node.
2. **Links** — The connecting path (wired or wireless) between nodes is known as a link.
3. **Data Sharing** — This is the primary purpose of a computer network.

---

## Why are Networks Important?

There are 4 primary functions that explain why networks are important:

1. **Resource Sharing** — Enables devices to share hardware and software resources.
2. **Communication** — Instant messaging, emails, phone calls, and video calls become easy.
3. **Data Access** — Files and documents can be accessed throughout the network.
4. **Collaboration** — Many users can collaborate and work together even when they are miles away.

---

## Types of Networks

There are 2 main types:

- **LAN** (Local Area Network)
- **WAN** (Wide Area Network)

---

## How LANs Work with WANs

LANs connect with WANs to access a broader network. For instance, when a home LAN is connected to an Internet Service Provider (ISP), all devices in the home network are provided with internet access.

A **modem** (Modulator–Demodulator) plays a vital role here. It acts as a bridge between the WAN and LAN by converting digital signals from the router into a format suitable for transmission over various media.
